Local SEO in the AI Era: Getting Found When Customers Ask ChatGPT for Recommendations

May 4, 2026

Customers are skipping Google and asking ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google's AI Overviews where to shop, eat, and hire. Here's how to make sure your business shows up.

The Way Customers Search Has Changed

Not long ago, getting found locally meant ranking on the first page of Google. That is still important, but it is no longer the whole picture. A growing share of your potential customers now open ChatGPT, Perplexity, or Google's built in AI Overviews and type something like "best accountant near me" or "which plumber in Austin has the best reviews?" They expect a direct, confident answer, not a list of blue links to sort through.

This shift is sometimes called search everywhere optimization, the practice of making sure your business is visible wherever people ask questions, not just on traditional search engines. If you have been focused only on classic SEO tactics, you may already be losing customers to competitors who appear in AI generated recommendations without even realizing it.

The good news is that the fundamentals that earn AI citations are not mysterious. They are extensions of good business hygiene, applied with a clearer understanding of how AI systems decide who to trust.

Why AI Recommends Some Businesses and Ignores Others

AI tools like ChatGPT and Perplexity do not browse the web in real time the way a person does. They are trained on large bodies of text and, in some cases, given the ability to retrieve live search results to supplement their answers. In both scenarios, they favor businesses that appear credible, consistent, and well documented across multiple sources.

Think of it this way: if twenty different websites, review platforms, and directories all say the same consistent things about your business, an AI system reads that as a strong signal of legitimacy. If your name, address, and phone number are inconsistent across platforms, or if your online presence is thin, the AI simply lacks enough evidence to recommend you confidently.

Research from McKinsey's QuantumBlack team consistently finds that companies with stronger data foundations outperform peers in customer acquisition and retention. The same principle applies at the local level: cleaner, richer business data earns more visibility in AI powered results.

The specific factors that matter most include:

  • Consistent business information across Google, Yelp, Facebook, Apple Maps, and industry directories
  • Volume and recency of reviews, especially those that mention specific services or locations
  • Clear, structured content on your own website that answers common customer questions directly
  • Authority signals such as mentions in local news, chamber of commerce listings, and reputable directories

Your Google Business Profile Is Still the Foundation

Even in the AI era, your Google Business Profile remains one of the most powerful pieces of real estate you control. Google's own AI Overviews pull heavily from this profile when answering local queries, so treating it as a set and forget checkbox is a costly mistake.

A strong profile today means:

  • Every field is filled in completely, including business hours, service areas, and a detailed description that uses natural language your customers actually speak
  • Photos are updated regularly, ideally showing your team, your space, and your work in progress
  • You respond to every review, positive or negative, within a few days
  • You use the Posts feature to share updates, offers, and seasonal announcements at least twice a month

That last point matters more than most owners realize. AI systems that access live Google data can see whether a business is actively maintained or dormant. An active, well tended profile signals a business that is open, engaged, and worth recommending.

Writing Content That AI Systems Are Happy to Quote

This is where local SEO AI strategy and content strategy converge. When an AI answers "who is the best family dentist in Raleigh," it often pulls a phrase or sentence directly from a business's website or review. That is called a citation, and earning citations consistently is one of the clearest competitive advantages available to small and mid sized businesses right now.

To write citation ready content, keep these principles in mind:

  1. Answer specific questions directly. Create short FAQ sections on your website that mirror the exact phrasing customers use when asking an AI. "Do you offer same day appointments?" is a better heading than "Our Scheduling Philosophy."
  2. Name your location explicitly and often. Do not assume the AI knows where you are. Mention your city, neighborhood, and service area naturally throughout your pages.
  3. Use plain language. AI systems, like human readers, prefer clear sentences over industry jargon. Short paragraphs with one idea each are easier to extract and cite accurately.
  4. Update content regularly. Stale content is a trust signal in the wrong direction. A blog post from 2019 with no updates suggests a business that may no longer be active.

You do not need a large content team to do this well. Even one focused page per quarter, written to answer a real customer question, compounds over time into a meaningful body of citable material.

Reviews Are Now Your Most Valuable Marketing Asset

When a customer asks an AI for a local recommendation, reviews are often the deciding factor. AI tools synthesize review sentiment to judge whether a business reliably delivers on its promises. A business with 200 reviews averaging 4.7 stars and specific mentions of quality and responsiveness will almost always outrank a competitor with 12 reviews and vague praise.

Building a strong review profile does not require tactics that violate platform guidelines. It requires a simple, consistent process:

  • Ask every satisfied customer for a review at the moment they are happiest, typically right after a great experience
  • Send a follow up text or email with a direct link to your Google review page so the process takes less than a minute
  • When you respond to reviews, use natural language that includes your service type and location, since those responses are also indexed and read by AI systems

The goal is not just a high rating. It is a rich, specific, recent body of social proof that gives an AI system plenty of confident language to draw from when it describes your business to a potential customer.

Where to Start If You Feel Behind

If all of this feels like a large project, start with an audit. Check that your business name, address, and phone number are identical on every platform where you appear. Fix any inconsistencies. Then look at your Google Business Profile with fresh eyes and ask whether a stranger reading it for the first time would have every question answered.

From there, pick one customer question per month and write a short, direct answer on your website. Build a simple process for requesting reviews. These are not technical tasks. They are disciplined habits that, over six to twelve months, create a compounding local SEO AI advantage that is genuinely difficult for competitors to replicate quickly.

The businesses that will win local AI recommendations are not necessarily the largest or the oldest. They are the ones that make it easy for AI systems to find clear, consistent, trustworthy information about who they are and what they do.
